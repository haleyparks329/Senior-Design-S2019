Training Methods

Multiple methods can be used to classify heartbeats, including the Naive Bayesian classifier, support vector machines (SVM), decision trees, and random forest. The Naive Bayes classifier and decision tree methods are examined and discussed on how they can be beneficial to this project.

Naive Bayesian classifiers are used for multiple classification problems, including everything from text classification to audio classification. Bayesian classifiers are optimal classifiers that minimize the average probablity of error; they are based on the assumption that previously known probablities and distributions of patterns are able to assign classes with one or more of the known probablities and patterns. A Naive Bayes classifier assumes that there is some independence between every pair of features. After every epoch, the classifier adjusts the calculation of likelihood probablities.

Decision trees are a simple and easily intrepreted classification method. The method leaerns simple decision rules based on the features to predict the class of the target. How the trees are split are based on criterion scores. Decisions trees are a white box model, meaning that a decision can be easily expained using boolean logic. Decision trees are a good fit for classifying heartbeats because of three main reasons: 1) do not need a lot of data; 2) can handle noisy data; and 3) can classify classes where its data points could be in multiple clusters in a feature space.
